Typing
Good typists aren't always good programmers, but bad typists typically do have more trouble learning to program. We've observed this phenomenon and our theory is that if you can't type the brackets, parentheses, periods, commas and other symbols needed for many programming languages from muscle memory, your brain struggles to load enough "context" to be a good programmer
